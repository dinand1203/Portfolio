Thermal AI

Graduation Project: Making AI's Environmental Impact Tangible

Role: Designer, Developer & Researcher Tools: JavaScript, Node.js, React, Arduino, Replit Period: January 2025 - September 2025

Research Question

"How can an embodied experience be designed to raise awareness among users of AI applications (aged 20‚Äì30) about the negative environmental impact, with the aim of encouraging meaningful reflection on their own AI usage?"

Project Overview

Artificial Intelligence often feels like magic: effortless and invisible. But behind every prompt and every generated image lies a growing ecological cost in energy and water. This project, Thermal AI, dives beneath the surface of our digital tools. It explores how we can translate the abstract environmental impact of AI into a direct, physical experience in order to stimulate deeper awareness and reflection.

The Problem

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become an indispensable part of our lives. Yet behind the interfaces of tools like ChatGPT and Midjourney lies an invisible world of immense energy consumption. These ecological costs remain abstract and unknown for most users. For instance, a single question to ChatGPT can consume up to 100 times more energy than a normal Google search.

The project focuses on young adults (20‚Äì30 years old), a target group that uses AI on a daily basis but often doesn't know the concrete environmental consequences of their digital behaviour. The core problem is that the ecological footprint of AI ‚Äì from training models to executing a simple prompt ‚Äì is completely invisible, which stands in the way of awareness.

Design Solution

The design solution is Thermal AI: a prototype that translates the abstract environmental impact of AI into a direct, physical experience (embodied experience). The system consists of a thermal mouse pad that increases in temperature as AI use intensifies, supported by an interface that visualizes this process.

When a user performs a task, they literally feel the energy impact of their digital action as warmth on their forearm. A complex task, such as generating an image, results in a fast and intense rise in temperature, while a simple question causes a more subtle warmth.

Through this direct connection, the user becomes intuitively ‚Äì and confrontationally ‚Äì aware of the energy their digital actions consume. Thermal AI is not just a technological tool, but also an instrument for reflection. The design invites users to think about their own AI behaviour and encourages a more sustainable way of engaging with the technology that is increasingly shaping our daily lives.
